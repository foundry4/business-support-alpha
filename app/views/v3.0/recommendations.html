Early stage businesses

{% if business.stageIndex==0 %}

Because you’re business is under three years old, you could benefit from support with getting started.

{% endif %}

When you’re at the beginning of your business journey, there’s a lot to think about. From setting up operations legally and hiring the right people to drawing up a sensible business plan, it’s easy for things to slip your mind

At this stage, help is available in many forms, with government-funded services on hand to give you the best possible chance of success.

We think you’ll be interested in the following services:

  • Financial support from Start Up Loans
    The Start Up Loans scheme is backed by the government. It offers free mentoring and low-interest personal loans of up to £25,000.
    Find out more

  • Advice straight to your inbox from Enterprise Nation
    Sign up for an email-only seven-day course, filled with practical advice and tips for starting your business.
    Find out more

Frequently asked questions

  • Which business set-up is right for me?

  • Can I speak to somebody for more help?
    {% if business.country=="England" %} Business Support Helpline (England) {% endif %} {% if business.country=="Scotland" %} Business Gateway (Scotland) {% endif %} {% if business.country=="Wales" %} Business Wales Helpline {% endif %} {% if business.country=="Northern Ireland" %} Invest Northern Ireland {% endif %} has a helpline that you can ring to speak to an advisor and get support with the essentials.
    Find out more

Mature businesses

{% if business.stageIndex==1 %}

Because you’ve been trading for {{ business.age }} years and your turnover is about £{{ business.turnover }}, you could benefit from support with boosting your business.

{% endif %}

Once you’ve been operating for a while, it becomes easy to stick to the processes you know and trust to produce results. But the outside world is always changing, and the success of your business depends on your ability to change with it.

Perhaps your productivity could be better, achieving more with the same resources. It might be time to tap into a new market, or take on apprentices to bring fresh talent into the workforce. Whatever boost you choose, there are government-backed schemes to support you.

We think you’ll be interested in the following services:

  • Productivity benchmarking from Be the Business
    Productivity directly affects the performance of your business. See how your productivity compares with similar companies, and learn how to improve it.
    Find out more

  • Exporting opportunities at great.gov.uk
    Overseas buyers are looking for products and services from the UK. Use this tool to find the right export opportunities for your business.
    Find out more

  • Online masterclasses with Enterprise Nation
    Subject-matter experts and successful entrepreneurs share their advice on topics such as upskilling staff and increasing sales. Browse a large library of online masterclasses.
    Find out more

Boost your business with people

  • Connect with a mentor
    Mentorsme is a gateway for quality-assured mentoring organisations across the UK. Find a mentor with experience, skills and contacts that will benefit you and your business.
    Find out more

  • Employ an apprentice
    Build a team of motivated, skilled individuals. The government offers financial support to small businesses offering apprenticeships.
    Find out more

Fast growing businesses

{% if business.stageIndex==2 %}

Because your business is growing rapidly and has a turnover greater than £200k, you could benefit from services that support growing, scaling and expanding.

{% endif %}

Growth is great for your business but it can create a lot of pressure. Your business needs to stay balanced in order to sustain any growth: you can’t serve ten times as many customers with the same number of products.

During this phase, financial support may be what your business needs most. Other options include focusing on innovation and beginning to work with the public sector.

We think you’ll be interested in the following services:

  • Finance support from the British Business Bank
    Equity crowdfunding or angel investor? Invoice financing or sector-specific grant? Use the Finance Hub to learn about finance options and understand which is best for your business.
    Find out more

  • Public sector contract advice from AXA
    The government is committed to awarding public sector contracts to small businesses, and AXA has guidance on how to research, price and write winning bids.
    Find out more

  • Funding and loans from Innovate UK
    Innovate UK is a government-backed agency that supports businesses with loans and funding worth up to £10 million. Read a detailed overview of how to apply
    Find out more

  • Mentorship programmes from Be the Business
    A fast-growing business needs a proven mentor, and Be the Business works with companies such as McKinsey & Company, Amazon and EY. Benefit from a mentor’s experience, network and contacts.
    Find out more
